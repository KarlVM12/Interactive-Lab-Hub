# FALL 2025 FAQ

Track Q&A for the labs from questions asked in the Interactive Device Design slack channel and during office hours. 

## Lab 1 Part 1

Q: How many people in a group?
A: I recommend 3-ish. It’s hard to do the assignment alone, and maybe hard to come up with enough for everyone to do with 5 people. But you decide

Q: I remember there used to be a link where we could view past students’ submissions on the Interactive-Lab-Hub, but I can’t seem to find it now. Could you remind me where to look? Thanks a lot!
A: A: You can look at who forked the lab and scroll down to previous years (2 years ago) forks.

Q: Do all storyboards have to be for the same idea?
A: No, it is fine to explore different ideas or settings

Q: Should each person make 7 storyboards?
A: We do not expect that a group of 3 people will make 21 storyboards, but we might expect more variety/quality.

Q: Does the device have to be one light, because that is what the smartphone prototyping tool Tinkerbell supports?
A: You can have multiple lights! Change Tinkerbelle's code to support your idea or prototype with other tools if needed!

Q: Does the storyboard have to be hand drawn? Can we use online images?
A1: A: No, it does not. Sketching on tablets or assembling storyboards using Photoshop or other tools is also fine! From previous years, we know that hand-drawn methods remain the most intuitive for exploring and communicating ideas.
If you use someone else's assets, ensure you have the rights to use them and attribute them properly. If you use AI, we will not accept a storyboard where the entire submission is generated in one shot.
A2: The storyboard does not need to be hand-drawn. It could be collaged, AI-generated, etc, etc.
Traditionally, storyboards have at least 3 cells and are 2D.

Q: Hi I noticed there’s zoom links in Canvas. Are those available for attending lectures online?
A1: There will be no Zoom links for attending remotely as this is an in-person class.
A2: Howdy! This is just a reminder not to ask me or the teaching team what happened when you weren’t in class. You should have contact information for other classmates to ask them.

Q: For the sketches , is it okay is it’s half digital? Or does it need to be consistent
A: That is fine ✍️

Q: For the video sketch of 3 interactions, should each person in a group be making 3 different videos? Or should it be around 3 in total?
A: You should collaborate on 3 videos. Every person should be involved.
Q: Would you be open to 1 video if interactions are not only based on the current input but also the accumulative state of previous interactions? For example, in the recording of our group, light state is not only dependent on people entering and exiting but also the number of people in the club, and shooting it in one set help with illustrating the state transition. Also, we are a large group, so not everyone ended up in the video, but everyone was involved in planning and shooting of the recording. Lmk if you would be cool with that.
A: When it says 3 videos, it is because we want a certain amount of exploration.
The assignment puts forth minimal benchmarks indicate exploration. With a large group, low exploration is even less acceptable.

Q: Hi Hauke, sorry to bother you during weekend. I'm writing to ask if I can get one more day for lab1 since it's holiday tmr and there are still some confusion and question going on in the slack chat.
A: Hi, no extensions to any assignments except for extraordinary circumstances
But as we said in class, the grading will always be for the whole Lab (parts 1 & 2) together.

Q: do the videos need to include the costumes?
A: Well, the videos should not feature phones as phones, since the point is phones as prototypes of interactive lights, but I guess the phones could be hidden without a costume…

Q: Do we have to make sketches and costumes for just 3 of the devices?
A: Yes, make sketches and costumes for 3 devices.

Q: I have trouble understanding the goal here. Is it 3 devices or 3 costumes for the same device?
A: 3 sketches or photos of devices in costume, different costumes

Q: I were confused on if we needed to physically create the different costumes for the device (or, just create sketches as the lab's README says). We've come up with a plethora of ideas and just wanted to confirm since we don't have any of the "hardware" on hand to create our device.
A: Physically create costumes.

Q: i Hauke, I have a question regarding part D of lab 1.

Show the follow-up work here.
Not clear what this means - does it mean changing the usecase/goal for the interaction?

### Lab1 Part 2
Q: will the second part of the lab be refining our initial device or coming up with a new device. My group partner and I came up with some new ideas and wanted to double check before running too far.
A: Ideally, you should focus on enhancing your project by reflecting on the insights gained from the previous weeks of storyboarding, acting out your concepts and talking to classmates.
If through this process, you realize that your initial ideas were not aligned with your vision it may be better to look for for new ideas.
